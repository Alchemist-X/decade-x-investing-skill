# Megatrends Checklist

> **Educational only. Not financial advice. Investing involves risk of loss.**

Use this checklist to evaluate whether a theme qualifies as a durable secular megatrend or a cyclical/narrative-driven fad. Work through every category. Document your answers.

---

## Category 1 — Demographics

A demographic megatrend is driven by changes in population size, age structure, or migration that are largely locked in for the next decade.

Prompts:
- Is this driven by an aging population, urbanization, or a growing middle class in a specific region?
- Are the relevant cohorts (consumers, workers, patients) increasing or decreasing in number — by how much, over what horizon?
- What UN or government demographic data supports this? What does the pessimistic demographic scenario look like?

**Disconfirmation prompt:** What demographic reversal (birth rate spike, migration policy change, life-extension breakthrough) would undermine this trend within 10 years?

Score: `__/5` (0 = not demographic, 3 = partially demographic, 5 = strongly demographic with locked-in data)

---

## Category 2 — Energy Transition

Prompts:
- Is this thesis tied to the shift from fossil fuels to electrification, hydrogen, or efficiency?
- What is the current cost curve trajectory for the enabling technology (solar, battery, grid storage)? Is it following Wright's Law?
- What does the incumbent energy industry do in response? How much capital is directed at defense?

**Disconfirmation prompt:** Under what policy reversal, technology plateau, or resource constraint scenario does this trend stall or reverse?

Score: `__/5`

---

## Category 3 — Compute and AI

Prompts:
- Is this thesis tied to continued Moore's Law dynamics, AI capability scaling, or semiconductor demand?
- What is the current utilization rate of the enabling infrastructure? Is this a build-out phase or a digestion phase?
- Has this sector shown a pattern of boom-bust capital cycles (see: telecom 1999, cloud 2021)?

**Disconfirmation prompt:** What regulatory, geopolitical (chip export controls), or architectural shift (alternative computing paradigms) could disrupt the assumed trajectory?

Score: `__/5`

---

## Category 4 — Biotechnology and Healthcare

Prompts:
- Is the thesis grounded in platform technology (mRNA, CRISPR, GLP-1 mechanisms) with expanding applications, or a single product/approval?
- What is the regulatory pathway risk? What does the historical base rate of clinical trial success look like in this indication?
- Is the payer dynamic (insurance, government, out-of-pocket) supportive over 10 years?

**Disconfirmation prompt:** What safety signal, patent cliff, or biosimilar dynamic would materially compress the opportunity within 5 years?

Score: `__/5`

---

## Category 5 — Geopolitical and Supply Chain Restructuring

Prompts:
- Is this driven by reshoring, friend-shoring, or decoupling trends with bipartisan/multi-government support?
- How much of the trend is already priced into capex announcements vs. actual shovel-in-ground activity?
- Are there infrastructure dependencies (skilled labor, permitting, raw materials) that could delay or kill this thesis?

**Disconfirmation prompt:** What geopolitical rapprochement or policy reversal would re-globalize the supply chains your thesis depends on?

Score: `__/5`

---

## Category 6 — Financial / Macro Tailwinds

Be cautious here. Financial tailwinds (low rates, credit expansion, multiple expansion) are cyclical, not secular.

Prompts:
- Does your thesis depend on a sustained low-rate environment? If rates normalize or rise, does the thesis survive?
- Are you confusing a long bull market in an asset class with a structural megatrend?
- What was the base rate of return for this asset class over the previous 30 years, across multiple rate cycles?

**Disconfirmation prompt:** If interest rates are 2 percentage points higher in 5 years, does the thesis still hold?

Score: `__/5` (high score = NOT rate-dependent; low score = heavily rate-dependent — lower is worse here)

---

## Fad Detection Prompts

Run these regardless of category:

1. **Narrative test:** Can you explain this trend to a skeptic using only data, not story? If the explanation requires "everyone knows that..." or "it's obvious that..." — flag it.
2. **Crowding test:** What percentage of investor surveys, fund flows, and media coverage are already bullish on this theme? High crowding reduces forward return expectations.
3. **History test:** Has this theme appeared before under a different name and disappointed? (E.g., "cleantech 1.0" in 2007–2009.)
4. **Supply response test:** If this opportunity is so large, why isn't capital flooding in to compete it away? If it is flooding in — what is the timeline to oversupply?
5. **Time horizon test:** Would this trend have been predicted confidently 10 years ago? If not, why is a 10-year prediction reliable now?
