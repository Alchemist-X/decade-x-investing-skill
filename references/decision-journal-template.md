# Decision Journal Template

> **Educational only. Not financial advice. Investing involves risk of loss.**

Copy and complete this template for each investment thesis you research using this framework. The purpose is to record your reasoning at the time of analysis so that future reviews can distinguish between: (a) the thesis breaking — which may warrant action, and (b) normal volatility testing your patience — which usually warrants no action.

Accurate post-mortem requires accurate pre-mortem documentation. The journal is only useful if you are honest — record your actual reasoning, not the reasoning you wish you had.

**Time budget:** A thorough journal entry should take 2–4 hours to complete. If it takes 20 minutes, you are probably summarizing rather than thinking.

- **How did this idea come to your attention?** (Friend's tip / media / analyst report / own research / screener output / other)

*Why this matters:* Idea source correlates with bias. Ideas that came through media hype or social proof require extra skepticism. Ideas from your own primary research get a slight discount for confirmation bias (you found what you were looking for). Tracking this reveals patterns in your sourcing quality over time.
