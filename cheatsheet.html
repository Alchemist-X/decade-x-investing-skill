Step 3 · Moat Types Reference
3 Moat Type Evidence
  • Switching costs — customer migration cost > stay cost; NRR >115%; multi-year contracts
  • Network effects — value per user grows with user count; winner-take-most dynamics; density matters
  • Cost advantage — structural (process, location, scale) not cyclical; sustainable at lower-volume
  • Intangible assets — patent life remaining; brand with demonstrated pricing power through recession
  • Efficient scale — market too small for viable second entrant; regional utility logic
Base Rates — ROIC Persistence
  • ~50% of ROIC >20% companies sustain it for 5+ years
  • ~25% sustain it for 10+ years
  • Declining gross margin = moat erosion signal
  • ROIC trending toward WACC = moat narrowing

Time-Horizon Matching Test
  • 10-yr thesis → funded only with capital not needed for 10+ yrs
  • Position −60%: still liquid for 2+ yrs living expenses without selling?
  • Position −60%: can hold / add — or will you sell at the bottom?
  • If daily price-checking causes anxiety → position is too large
Correlation Traps
  • 5 AI semiconductor stocks ≠ diversification (correlation ≈ 1.0 in risk-off)
  • Long-duration bonds + long-duration growth stocks = rate risk concentration
  • Multiple "different" thematic ETFs sharing top-10 holdings
  • Stress test: if top theme −40%, what does whole portfolio do?

Core Principles
∞ The Five Pillars
  • Process over predictions.
    10-year forecasts are almost always wrong in specifics. Good process is more controllable than outcome prediction. Document the process so you can improve it.
  • Base rates first.
    Before building any bull case, ask: what fraction of companies in this situation historically succeeded? Most disruption stories, turnarounds, and super-cycles disappoint.
  • Seek disconfirmation.
    Spend at least as much time looking for evidence your thesis is wrong as evidence it is right. A thesis that cannot be falsified is a belief, not an analysis.
  • Second-order effects matter.
    The first-order effect is usually already priced in. What happens to suppliers, competitors, regulators, and consumer behavior downstream is where insight lives.
  • Humility is a feature.
    Express views as probability ranges, not certainties. Document key assumptions explicitly so you can track which ones broke — not which ones you rationalized after the fact.
Quick-Start Checklist
  • Thesis stated in ≤2 sentences before any research
  • Megatrend score ≥14/30 (or extraordinary evidence documented)
  • All 6 durability dimensions scored; no dimension at 0–1
  • Reverse DCF run; implied growth rate placed in historical distribution
  • Three scenarios with explicit probabilities; expected value computed
  • Margin of safety matches confidence level
  • Position size reflects durability score AND valuation margin
  • Exit conditions written before entry
  • Review dates in calendar before entry
  • Devil's advocate section written without softening
